💡 Math Tips!

🧮 What is an Array?

An array is objects arranged in rows (across ↔) and columns (up and down ↕)!

Example: 3 rows × 4 columns
🔴🔴🔴🔴 ← row 1
🔴🔴🔴🔴 ← row 2
🔴🔴🔴🔴 ← row 3
Point to each dot and count them all yourself!

💡 Try skip counting by the number in each row!

If each row has 4 dots, skip count by 4. Say one number for each row — stop when you've counted every row. The last number you say is your answer!

💰 Coin Values!

🟤 Penny = 1¢ ($0.01)
⚪ Nickel = 5¢ ($0.05)
⚫ Dime = 10¢ ($0.10)
🔘 Quarter = 25¢ ($0.25)
💵 Dollar = 100¢ ($1.00)

Start with the biggest coin first when counting!

Example: Quarter + Dime = ?
A quarter is 25¢. A dime is 10¢. Start at 25 and count up 10 more. What number do you reach?

🛒 Smart Shopping!

Think: Do I need this or just want it?

A higher price doesn't always mean better! Compare before you buy!

Always check you have enough money first!

📚 Math Vocabulary

Array

Objects arranged in equal rows and columns. Arrays help us multiply!

Row

A line going across (left to right ↔). Like seats in a row at the movies!

Column

A line going up and down (↕). Like pillars holding up a building!

Multiplication

Adding equal groups together. 3 × 4 means "3 groups of 4." Arrays show this!

Factor

Numbers you multiply together. In 3 × 4 = 12, the factors are 3 and 4.

Product

The answer when you multiply. In 3 × 4 = 12, the product is 12.

Skip Counting

Counting by a number other than 1. Like: 5, 10, 15, 20 (skip counting by 5)!

Penny

Worth 1 cent. It takes 100 pennies to make $1.00!

Nickel

Worth 5 cents. Equals 5 pennies!

Dime

Worth 10 cents. Even though it's the smallest coin, it's worth more than a nickel!

Quarter

Worth 25 cents. Four quarters make $1.00!

Dollar

Worth 100 cents. You can pay with a dollar bill!

Total

The full amount when you add everything together.

Change

The money you get BACK when you pay more than something costs.
